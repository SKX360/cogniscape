


RCA: A sudden drop in chamber pressure in a solid rocket motor followed by trajectory Deviation

Mission Termination:  ISRO terminated the mission to ensure safety, resulting in the loss of the EOS-09 satellite.

Specific Observations
a. Sudden loss in Chamber Pressure [Telemetry showed a pressure drop within ~1 second of third-stage ignition]

Observations
External 
a. Deviation in Trajectory:  The rocket veered off its intended path during the third-stage burn, indicating a potential issue with thrust vector control. 
-Radar and other external monitoring systems and internal telemetry data



Internal Telemetry Systems
a. Loss of chamber pressure 
a. Underperformance of Third-Stage Motor: Insufficient velocity for orbital insertion. 

Caused By
a. Loss of thrust was caused by lost of chamber pressure



Unknown
a. The nozzle was expected to pivot for attitude correction - did that happen [Irrelevant because loss-of-thrust would be sufficient to cause trajectory deviation irrespective of whether the flex Nozzle pivoted for attitude correction or not]


Explanation
1. Loss of Chamber pressure 
2. lead to lost of thrust 
3. Underperformance of Third-Stage Motor 
4. insufficient velocity for orbital insertion








Context
Solid rocket motors do not rely on active fuel flow systems like liquid engines. Instead, they use pre-packed solid propellant that burns from the inside out in a controlled manner.

The fuel didn’t stop flowing, because solid motors don’t have active pumps or flow lines.

The combustion didn’t "taper off" gently — a sudden drop suggests a mechanical fault, not a graceful degradation.

 Why This Is Important:
Chamber pressure in solid motors is a proxy for combustion efficiency and nozzle behavior.

A drop means something disrupted the balance between burning propellant and gas expansion through the nozzle.


Seal or Joint Failure
Extreme or abrupt misalignment could physically damage internal seals or the flexible joint.

This might cause hot gas leaks — reducing internal pressure without visible external damage until after failure.
